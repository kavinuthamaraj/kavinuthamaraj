  <h3 style="font-size: 1.2em; margin-top: 1em; margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Universiti Malaya (UM), Malaysia</h3>
  <p style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;"><em>Quantum Machine Learning Research Intern</em></p>
  <p style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;"><em>June 2025 – July 2025</em></p>
  <ul style="margin-left: 2em; margin-bottom: 1em;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Under Prof. Vannajan Sanghiran Lee, Head of Centre of Excellence UM Quantum Information Science and Technology (CoE UMQiST) at Universiti Malaya, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Project: Hybrid Quantum-Classical U-Net for Brain Tumor Segmentation.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Developed and optimized a Classical U-Net and a Hybrid Quantum Neural Network (HQNN) for brain tumor segmentation on the BraTS2020 dataset, processing 51,475 multi-modal MRI scans (T1, T1c, T2, FLAIR, 240×240×155).</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Achieved 92.35% validation IoU and 0.0704 DiceLoss with Classical U-Net (5.50M parameters), and 90.38% IoU and 0.0936 DiceLoss with HQNN (2.26M parameters), using a 90:10 train-validation split (46,328 train, 5,147 val).</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Engineered HQNN by integrating quantum circuits into the bottleneck layer, reducing parameters by 59% (2.26M vs 5.50M) while maintaining competitive IoU (90.38% vs. 92.35%), demonstrating quantum efficiency.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Implemented per-class IoU metrics for necrosis, edema, and enhancing tumor.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Generated learning curves and efficiency plots, revealing HQNN’s superior IoU/parameter ratio (4.00e-07 vs 1.68e-07), highlighting potential for resource-constrained medical imaging applications.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0.5em;">Tools & Technologies: PennyLane, PyTorch, MONAI, Dice Score, nnUNet, Streamlit.</li>
  </ul>
